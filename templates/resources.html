Prescription assistance can be offered in the way of Patient Assistance Programs (PAPs), which are created by pharmaceutical companies to provide free or discounted medicines to people who are unable to afford them. Each program has its own qualifying criteria. To apply, go directly to the pharmaceutical website and look for a link related to patient discount or patient assistance programs.


Susan G Komen Treatment Assistance Program

This page will offer you individual assessment by an oncology social worker. It will provide financial assistance for: Medication, Child Care/Elder Care, Transportation, Durable Medical Equipment, Breast Cancer Education, Psychosocial Support and Local Resources.

Patient Advocate Foundation

Easy application online. This foundation will provide copayment assistance to commercially insured patients. In order to qualify, patient must meet requirements such as:

  • Patient must be insured, and insurance must cover the medication for which patient seeks assistance.
  • Patient must have a confirmed diagnosis of Breast Cancer.
  • Patient must reside and receive treatment in the United States.
  • Patient's income must fall below 400% of the Federal Poverty Guideline (FPG) with consideration of the Cost of Living Index(COLI) and the number in the household.

CancerCare Co-Payment Assistance Foundation

CancerCare provides free, professional support services for people affected by breast cancer, as well as breast cancer information and additional resources.

Catherine H Tuck Foundation

This Program helps working women and men nationwide who are struggling to pay their bills during breast cancer treatment. We provide short-term aid by making payments for non-medical expenses for rent, utilities, food or transportation.
